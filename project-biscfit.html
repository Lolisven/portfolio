Brief

As part of a university project for my minor degree in marketing, I designed three packagings for cookies called Bisc-Fit. The packagings come in three flavors: chocolate, hazelnut, and strawberry. The task was to create a company name, packaging, and a business strategy for selling the cookies. My responsibility was to design the packaging for Bisc-Fit cookies.

Goals

The primary goal of designing the packaging for Bisc-Fit cookies was to create an attractive and eye-catching design that would make the product stand out on the shelves. The packaging needed to be visually appealing to the target audience, which was health-conscious individuals who are looking for a snack that is both delicious and nutritious.

Research

To create an effective packaging design, I conducted extensive research into the target audience, the competition, and current packaging trends. I studied the packaging designs of similar products and noted the color schemes, fonts, and visual elements that were commonly used. I also looked into the latest packaging trends and identified elements that would make the Bisc-Fit packaging modern and appealing to the target audience.

I also researched the health benefits of the ingredients used in Bisc-Fit cookies. This information helped me to create a packaging design that highlights the nutritional value of the product and its benefits for the consumer. By combining research on the target audience and the product, I was able to design a packaging that is not only visually appealing but also communicates that the cookies are the healthier option in the store.
