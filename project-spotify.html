Problem

During a conversation with my friends, I gathered valuable insights regarding their experience with the desktop app version of Spotify. The feedback I received was consistent in highlighting the challenges associated with navigating through the interface, which was perceived as being cluttered with too many elements.

In particular, users expressed frustration with the time it took to complete tasks on the app, which impacted their overall experience. This feedback was crucial in informing my redesign process, as I focused on streamlining the interface and simplifying the user journey to make it more intuitive and efficient.

Goals

Improve home screen infrastructure to simplify user flows and make key fuctions more accessible.

Research

As part of my redesign of Spotify's homescreen, I conducted a user research study with a sample of approximately 6 individuals, consisting of friends and family members. To ensure that my research findings were inclusive and representative, I deliberately targeted a few individuals aged 40 and above, who tend to have more difficulty navigating through digital interfaces.

Through a combination of one-on-one interviews and surveys, I gathered feedback from participants on their likes and dislikes of the current interface. This feedback was then compiled and analyzed to identify the key issues that were causing frustration and confusion for users.

To further inform my redesign, I conducted a thorough analysis of the user interfaces of Spotify's main competitors. This enabled me to identify best practices and innovative solutions that could be adapted to improve the user experience of Spotify's homescreen. Based on my research findings and analysis, I developed a series of wireframes and prototypes, which I tested with a small group of participants to gain feedback and refine my designs. The final result is a visually appealing and user-friendly homescreen that addresses the key pain points identified in the research.

Ideation

The ideation process involved generating and exploring a variety of potential solutions to address the pain points identified during the research phase. This was done through a collaborative and iterative process that involved brainstorming, sketching wireframes, and testing prototypes with users. The resulting designs effectively addressed the key pain points identified during the research phase. Ultimately, I decided that this one was the best of all of them.
