Problem

During a conversation with my friends, I gathered valuable insights regarding their experience with the desktop app version of Spotify. The feedback I received was consistent in highlighting the challenges associated with navigating through the interface, which was perceived as being cluttered with too many elements.

In particular, users expressed frustration with the time it took to complete tasks on the app, which impacted their overall experience. This feedback was crucial in informing my redesign process, as I focused on streamlining the interface and simplifying the user journey to make it more intuitive and efficient.

Goals

Improve home screen infrastructure to simplify user flows and make key fuctions more accessible.

Research

Based on my research findings, it seems that the current homescreen of Spotify's desktop app may be causing some frustration among its users. Users reported that the homescreen looked cluttered, which could be overwhelming and confusing.

Additionally, some users expressed a desire to have the Discover section separated from the Search section, as they found it difficult to navigate when these two features were combined.

Lastly, the fact that the Settings and Friends sections were located within the Account section was a pain point for users, as they wanted these features to be more easily accessible.

Overall, it seems that there is a need to streamline the layout and navigation of the desktop app to improve the user experience and make it more intuitive for users to find what they're looking for.

Color Schemes

In my opinion, Spotify's use of dark colors in their desktop app may not be as effective as they intended it to be. Through my research, I found that some users reported that the dark colors made the appearance look cluttered and disorienting, which could be a barrier to a positive user experience.

While dark color schemes can be visually striking and create a sense of depth, it seems that in this case it may have had unintended negative consequences. It's important to consider the balance between aesthetics and usability when designing an app, and to make sure that the visual design supports the functionality of the app.

In this case, it may be worth exploring a little bit lighter color schemes or design elements to create a more streamlined and user-friendly experience for users.

Ideation

The ideation process involved generating and exploring a variety of potential solutions to address the pain points identified during the research phase. This was done through a collaborative and iterative process that involved brainstorming and sketching wireframes. The resulting design effectively addressed the key pain points.
